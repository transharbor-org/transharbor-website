# Voting & Governance

TransHarbor is a democratically-governed, user-owned nonprofit platform.

## Legal Structure

Wyoming Unincorporated Nonprofit Association (UNA) — no private owners, shareholders, or investors. Operating a user-owned, democratically-governed community platform for trans and nonbinary people.

## Protected Principles

These core values require supermajority (2/3 + 30% quorum) to change:

1. **Trans and Nonbinary Centered** — Platform for and by trans and nonbinary people
2. **User Ownership** — Collectively owned, democratic governance
3. **Anti-Discrimination** — Zero tolerance for harassment or hate
4. **Privacy & Data Sovereignty** — Your data belongs to you, never sold
5. **Democratic Decision-Making** — Transparent voting processes

## Membership

**All Users:** Full platform access, Community Guidelines protection, ability to report violations, right to delete account and data.

**Voting Members** (automatic when qualified):
- Requirements: 3+ posts, 60+ days old, posted within last 60 days, no active moderation strikes
- Rights: Submit proposals, vote on decisions, shape platform policies
- Status updated daily, restored when requirements met again

## Voting Process

**Proposal Types:**
- Standard (simple majority): Features, policy changes, moderation guidelines
- Protected Principles (2/3 + 30% quorum): Core values, membership criteria, voting processes, privacy policy

**Submission:** Voting members only, one proposal per week

**Lifecycle:**
1. Discussion (7 days) — Community feedback, author may edit
2. Voting (7 days) — Anonymous votes (Yes/No/Abstain), can change vote
3. Resolution — Standard: majority wins; Protected: requires 2/3 + 30% participation
4. Implementation — Organizers execute with progress updates
