# Governance Structure

TransHarbor is a democratically-governed, user-owned nonprofit platform. This document explains how our community makes decisions together.

## Legal Foundation

### Wyoming Unincorporated Nonprofit Association (UNA)

TransHarbor operates as a Wyoming Unincorporated Nonprofit Association under Wyoming Statute ยง17-22-101 et seq. This structure provides:

- **No private ownership** - No shareholders, investors, or private owners
- **Nonprofit operation** - No profit distribution to individuals
- **Democratic governance** - Members participate in decision-making
- **Limited liability** - Members and organizers are protected from personal liability
- **Simplicity** - Minimal bureaucracy and regulatory burden
- **Flexibility** - Can evolve governance structures through member voting

## Protected Principles

These core values can only be changed by supermajority vote (2/3 of voting members + 30% quorum):

### 1. Trans and Nonbinary Centered
This platform exists for and is governed by trans and nonbinary people. While allies may participate respectfully, the platform's focus, priorities, and decision-making must center trans and nonbinary experiences and needs.

### 2. User Ownership and Cooperative Governance
TransHarbor is owned collectively by its users, not by private individuals, corporations, or investors. Active members have a meaningful voice in how the platform operates through democratic processes.

### 3. Anti-Discrimination and Safety
Zero tolerance for harassment, hate speech, discrimination, or behavior that threatens community safety. Safety and respect are non-negotiable priorities.

### 4. Privacy and Data Sovereignty
User data belongs to users, not to TransHarbor, advertisers, or third parties. We will never sell user data or compromise privacy for profit. Privacy protections cannot be weakened without supermajority approval.

### 5. Democratic Decision-Making
Important platform decisions are made through transparent, democratic processes where voting members participate. Governance structures must remain democratic and accountable to the community.

## Membership Structure

### All Users

Everyone who creates a TransHarbor account is a community member with:
- Full access to platform features (posts, messaging, profiles)
- Protection under Community Guidelines
- Ability to report violations
- Right to block other users
- Right to delete their account and data

### Voting Members

Active participants automatically become voting members when they meet these criteria:

**Requirements:**
- Posted at least 3 times on the platform
- Account at least 60 days old
- Posted within the last 60 days (actively participating)
- No active moderation strikes

**Rights:**
- Submit governance proposals
- Vote on all proposals
- Participate in discussion periods
- Shape platform policies and features
- Influence moderation guidelines

**Responsibilities:**
- Vote thoughtfully on proposals
- Participate in good faith
- Respect democratic outcomes
- Help maintain community standards

### Voting Membership Status

Status is **automatically calculated** and updated daily based on the criteria above. There is no application process or manual approval.

**Loss of voting membership:**
- Inactivity (no posts in 60 days) - automatically restored when posting resumes
- Moderation strikes - restored when strikes are removed or expire
- Account deletion - permanent

**Voting member badge:**
- Displayed on user profiles
- Indicates active participation and governance rights
- Updated automatically as status changes

## Democratic Governance Process

### Proposal Types

**Standard Proposals** (Simple majority)
- New features or improvements
- Non-critical policy changes
- Community event planning
- Moderation guideline adjustments
- Resource allocation

**Protected Principle Changes** (Supermajority)
- Changes to core values listed above
- Changes to voting membership criteria
- Changes to proposal/voting processes
- Major structural changes
- Privacy policy weakening

### Proposal Submission

**Who can submit:** Voting members only

**How to submit:**
1. Navigate to Governance section in app
2. Click "Submit Proposal"
3. Fill out proposal form:
   - Title (max 200 characters, clear and descriptive)
   - Description (min 100 characters, detailed explanation)
   - Category (governance, moderation, features, community guidelines, other)
   - Protected principle change? (checkbox - triggers supermajority requirement)

**Proposal requirements:**
- Must be clear and specific
- Must be feasible and within scope
- Must not violate protected principles (unless supermajority)
- Must be submitted in good faith

**Submission limits:**
- One proposal per voting member per week
- Prevents spam and allows thoughtful proposals

### Proposal Lifecycle

#### Phase 1: Discussion Period (7 days)

**Purpose:** Community discussion, feedback, and refinement

**During discussion:**
- Proposal is visible to all members
- Comments section for discussion (text only)
- Author may edit description (changes are tracked)
- Community provides feedback and suggestions
- Author can refine proposal based on feedback

**Discussion period ends:** Automatically after 7 days

#### Phase 2: Voting Period (7 days)

**Purpose:** Formal voting on the proposal

**Voting begins:** Immediately after discussion period ends

**Voting options:**
- **Yes** - Support the proposal
- **No** - Oppose the proposal
- **Abstain** - Counted for quorum but not for/against

**Vote visibility:**
- Individual votes are anonymous
- Aggregate counts visible in real-time
- Total voting members count displayed

**Vote changes:**
- Can change vote any time during voting period
- Final vote when period ends

**Notifications:**
- Voting begins (all voting members)
- 24-hour reminder (all voting members)
- Voting ends (all voting members)

#### Phase 3: Resolution

**Standard proposals:**
- **Pass:** More Yes votes than No votes
- **Fail:** More No votes than Yes votes
- **Tie:** Fails (requires clear majority)

**Protected principle changes:**
- **Pass:** 2/3 (66.67%) of votes cast are Yes AND at least 30% of total voting members participated
- **Fail:** Less than 2/3 Yes OR less than 30% participation

**Results display:**
- Yes votes (count and percentage)
- No votes (count and percentage)
- Abstain votes (count)
- Total votes cast
- Total voting members
- Pass/Fail status

**After resolution:**
- Status updated (passed or failed)
- All members notified of outcome
- Passed proposals move to implementation

#### Phase 4: Implementation (for passed proposals)

**Responsibility:** TransHarbor organizers/developers

**Process:**
1. Review technical feasibility and timeline
2. Post implementation plan as comment on proposal
3. Begin development work
4. Post progress updates
5. Announce when complete
6. Update proposal status to "Implemented"

**Timeline:** Varies by complexity
- Simple policy changes: Immediate to 1 week
- Feature additions: 2-12 weeks
- Major platform changes: 3-6 months

**Transparency:** Regular updates posted to proposal

## Organizer/Administrator Roles

### Bootstrap Phase (Until Democratic Structure Fully Operational)

During initial launch, a small organizing team manages:
- Technical infrastructure (hosting, deployment, updates)
- Critical security issues
- Emergency moderation (severe violations)
- Legal and financial compliance
- Implementation of passed proposals

**Organizing team:**
- [Names/roles TBD]
- Acts as stewards, not owners
- Subject to community oversight
- Committed to transitioning to democratic governance

### Long-Term Structure

As the platform grows, organizers become:
- **Implementers** of community decisions, not decision-makers
- **Facilitators** of the democratic process
- **Technical stewards** maintaining infrastructure
- **Accountable** to voting members

**Powers:**
- Emergency actions (security, severe abuse) - reported to community
- Technical implementation of passed proposals
- Day-to-day moderation (within established guidelines)
- Financial management (transparently reported)

**Limitations:**
- Cannot change protected principles without supermajority vote
- Cannot override democratic decisions
- Cannot access user data beyond necessary for platform operation
- Must report significant actions to community

## Moderation Governance

### Moderation Team

**Volunteer moderators:**
- Apply through community process
- Approved by organizers initially, then by voting members
- Follow established moderation guidelines
- Report actions transparently

**Moderator actions:**
- Review reported content
- Issue warnings and strikes
- Remove violating content
- Suspend/ban accounts (per guidelines)
- Escalate complex cases

### Accountability

- Moderation actions logged
- Monthly transparency reports
- Community can propose changes to moderation guidelines
- Serious moderator misconduct can trigger removal vote

### Appeals

- Users can appeal moderation decisions
- Email moderation@transharbor.org
- Reviewed by different moderator
- Response within 14 days
- Community can propose appeals process improvements

## Amendment Process

### Amending This Document

Changes to governance structure require:

**Standard changes:**
- Governance proposal
- Simple majority vote
- 7-day discussion, 7-day voting

**Changes to protected principles or voting requirements:**
- Governance proposal marked as protected principle change
- Supermajority vote (2/3 + 30% quorum)
- 14-day discussion, 7-day voting
- Higher bar reflects importance

### Living Document

This governance structure is designed to evolve with community needs. Regular review and improvement through democratic process is encouraged.

## Dissolution

In the unlikely event TransHarbor must dissolve:

### Process

1. Proposal submitted explaining reasons
2. Supermajority vote required (2/3 + 30% quorum)
3. 30-day notice to all members
4. Data export offered to all users
5. Assets distributed to similar nonprofit organizations
6. Domain and assets cannot be sold for profit

### Data Handling

- All users notified immediately
- 60 days to export data
- All user data permanently deleted
- Backups destroyed
- Open-source code remains available

## Conflict Resolution

### User-User Conflicts

- Encouraged to resolve directly when safe
- Use block feature as needed
- Report violations of guidelines
- Mediation available for serious ongoing conflicts

### User-Moderator Conflicts

- Appeal system (described above)
- Can propose moderation guideline changes
- Can submit governance proposals about moderation

### User-Organizer Conflicts

- Contact disputes@transharbor.org
- Mediation process
- Community can vote on organizer accountability measures
- Transition to elected organizers resolves long-term concerns
